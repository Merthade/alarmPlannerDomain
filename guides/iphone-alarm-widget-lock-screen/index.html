How to Put Your Next Alarm on the iPhone Lock Screen

Half the value of an alarm app is the two-second check: is something armed for tomorrow, or not? A widget answers that without opening anything.

Quick answer: Alarm Clock Planner ships Lock Screen and Home Screen widgets that show your next alarm. Long-press the Lock Screen, tap Customize, and add the widget to the strip under the clock. The Home Screen widget goes further: it can toggle an alarm without opening the app.

The two-second check

The "did I actually set it?" doubt usually strikes after you are already in bed. Unlocking the phone, finding the app, and reading the list is a small task at the worst time, and it wakes you up more than it should. A glanceable widget exists to end exactly that ritual.

Add it to the Lock Screen

  1. Press and hold the Lock Screen, then tap Customize.
  2. Choose the Lock Screen and tap the widget strip under the clock.
  3. Find Alarm Planner in the widget list and add the circular or rectangular widget.
  4. Tap Done. Your next alarm now sits on the Lock Screen, visible without unlocking.

Add it to the Home Screen

  1. Press and hold an empty area of the Home Screen.
  2. Use the add or Edit Widget control and search for Alarm Planner.
  3. Pick a size and drop it where you want it.

The Home Screen widget is interactive: the toggle on it arms or silences the alarm directly, no app launch needed.

What the widget shows

  • Your next alarm, by name and time, straight from your alarm list.
  • A countdown, which matters most for date-based alarms: an alarm three weeks out shows how many days remain, so you know it is armed without doing calendar math.
  • Your theme: the widget follows the app theme you picked, so it looks like it belongs on your Home Screen.
The widget stays in sync with your alarm list: create, skip, or toggle an alarm in the app and the widget reflects it.

Your next alarm, always one glance away

Lock Screen and Home Screen widgets included. Free for up to 3 alarms.
