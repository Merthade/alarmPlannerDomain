How to Skip an Alarm Once Without Turning It Off

Tomorrow is a holiday, so you toggle the 6:30 alarm off. The dangerous part comes two days later, when nothing rings, because nothing was ever switched back on.

Quick answer: The built-in Clock app has no skip-once option: your only move is to disable the alarm and remember to re-enable it. In Alarm Clock Planner, Skip next occurrence silences only the next ring of a repeating alarm and keeps the schedule armed, so the morning after the day off is protected automatically.

The toggle-and-forget trap

Turning a repeating alarm off for one day is easy. The cost is hidden in the follow-up task: turning it back on, a task assigned to future-you on the exact evening you are least likely to remember it. The failure mode is never the day off. It is the oversleep the morning after.

People have asked Apple for a skip function for years, and as of iOS 26 the Clock app still does not offer one. The alarm is either on, and will ring tomorrow, or off, and will never ring again until you remember it exists.

Skip the next occurrence, keep the schedule

In Alarm Clock Planner a repeating alarm has a third state:

  1. Open the repeating alarm you want to quiet down.
  2. Choose Skip next occurrence.
  3. That single ring is skipped. The one after it stays armed, untouched, no memory required.

The alarm card makes it clear a skip is pending, so you always know what will and will not ring.

When one skip is not enough

A whole week off is not a skip problem, it is a group problem. Tag your weekday alarms into a Work group and toggle the entire group off in one motion when vacation starts, then back on when it ends. Skip handles the single day; groups handle the season.
